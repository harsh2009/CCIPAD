Who I am
Fr. Ikechukwu Mathias Odigbo is interested in providing a practical answer to one central question: which social problems can the church help to solve? This has been his driving force in his involvement in humanitarian and charity projects.

Inspired by the rich resources of Catholic Social Teaching that call us to respond to social problems in practical ways as Jesus did, he believes that the church or the Christian Community can do a lot to end extreme poverty, exploitation of workers, abuse of human rights, untimely death, maltreatment of prisoners etc.

History and Experience
He has had the privilege of serving as the President of the Justice and Peace Association while in the seminary. Regular visits to orphanages and prisons exposed him to problems of human rights, incarceration, abuse of power and related issues of poverty and human suffering. After ordination, he also worked as Deputy Director at the Catholic Institute for Development Justice and Peace (CIDJAP) in Enugu and doubled up at the National level as member of the Justice and Development Commission (JDPC) of the Catholic Bishops Conference of Nigeria.

CIDJAP had activities in almost all areas of development like prisoners’ care, scholarships and education interventions, micro-credit for rural women and Small and Medium-scale Enterprises (SMEs), HIV/AIDs and tuberculosis, health advocacy etc. But while CIDJAP looked outwards to foreigners to help solve our local problems through funding and technical support, very little local initiatives by the indigenes themselves were doing anything. This seemed to me like a gap. I thought some problems are better solved looking inwards.

My Passion
In 2008, he founded the Christian Community Initiative for Peace and Development. Its primary mission is to empower Christian Communities at the parish, zonal, society and organisation levels to undertake projects that can bring development and progress within the communities. Although communities might benefit occasionally from foreign technical and financial support, the task of building a community which works for everyone is their primary responsibility.

This will mean their thinking together, working together as Christians to achieve defined objectives and goals. My passion is to help support the effort of Christians to work together and solve some of the most simple and basic problems that face them every day.

My Prayer
I implore leaders of various Christian communities like bishops, priests, pastors, and ministers of religion as well as lay members of the church who occupy leadership positions in the church to add an active social dimension, however small, to their ministry. We have the duty to encourage, teach and motivate the Christians to care for each other by solving those small problems that enhance good human life.

Clearly, our organisation CCIPAD cannot be everywhere to mobilize Christians to carry out developmental projects, but we can offer technical and advisory support. On this website and on our social media pages, we shall provide regular resources that will be useful to those who wish to contribute to the positive changes that come to communities when Christians and members of the communities or localities work together. You can contact us for further dialogue and collaborations.
